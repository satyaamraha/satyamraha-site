My Story

A curious mind, from circuits to cosmos

I have always wanted to know how things work — first machines, then code, and finally the deepest machine of all: human life itself.

The maker in me

From childhood I was fascinated by technology. I would dismantle TV remotes and fix them, repair table clocks, build bed switches in my room, and assemble computers with my own hands. Curiosity was never a hobby — it was instinct.

My journey into computer science began in the 7th standard. C programming found me in the 10th — though I confess I didn't truly understand coding then. What I did have was a hunger to keep going until logic finally clicked. And when it did, it never stopped.

The seeker in me

Life has its winters. I have walked through stages where I had nothing left to do but leave everything to God. It was in those moments that I turned to the Vedic sciences — not as superstition, but as a search for meaning that still had to make sense.

For me, computer science is a luxury, but the occult sciences are a necessity. When people face problems, they turn to a doctor, a lawyer, or an astrologer. I wanted to understand the third with the same logic I gave the first two.
